TITLE: Weight Loss: 6 Strategies for Success
SOURCE: Mayo Clinic
AUTHOR: Mayo Clinic Staff
DATE: June 22, 2024
========================================================================

Hundreds of fad diets, weight-loss programs and outright scams promise quick and easy weight loss. But the best way to lose weight and keep it off is to make lasting lifestyle changes — eating a balanced diet and moving more each day.

STRATEGY 1: MAKE SURE YOU'RE READY

Long-term weight loss takes time and effort. Ask yourself:
- Do I have a strong desire to change habits to help me lose weight?
- Am I willing to change my eating habits?
- Am I willing to change my physical activity and exercise habits?
- Do I use food to cope with stress?
- Do I need other support from friends or healthcare professionals?

Talk with your healthcare professional if you need help taking charge of stress. Lowering stress can help you make long-term healthy lifestyle changes.

STRATEGY 2: FIND YOUR INNER DRIVE

No one else can make you lose weight. You need to make diet and physical activity changes to help yourself. Make a list of reasons why weight loss is important to you — the list can help you stay inspired and focused.

Find people who will:
- Listen to your concerns and feelings.
- Share your goal to lead a healthy lifestyle.
- Do active hobbies with you or help you make healthy menus.

Track your diet and exercise in a journal or an app. Also track your weight. Review your progress and make changes as needed.

STRATEGY 3: SET GOALS YOU CAN REACH

Aim to lose 1 to 2 pounds (0.5 to 1 kilogram) a week over the long term. To do that, you'll need to burn about 500 to 750 calories more than you take in each day.

Losing 5% of your current weight may be a good goal to start with. Example: If you weigh 180 pounds, that's 9 pounds. Even this amount of weight loss can lower your risk of heart disease and type 2 diabetes.

Set two types of goals:
- Action goal: "Walk every day for 30 minutes" — a healthy action you'll use to lose weight.
- Outcome goal: "Lose 10 pounds" — the result you aim to achieve.

STRATEGY 4: ENJOY HEALTHY FOODS

To lose weight, lower the total calories from food and drinks. One way to take in fewer calories is to eat more fruits, vegetables, and whole grains — low in calories and high in fiber. Fiber helps you feel full.

Diet tips:
- Eat at least four servings of vegetables and three servings of fruits a day.
- Choose whole grains: brown rice, barley, whole-wheat bread and pasta. Limit refined grains (white rice, white bread).
- Use healthy fats: olive oil, vegetable oils, avocados, nuts, nut butters. Keep in mind that even healthy fats are high in calories.
- Limit foods and drinks with added sugar (desserts, jellies, sodas).
- Choose low-fat or fat-free dairy products.
- Focus on fresh foods — they have more nutrition than processed foods.

Be mindful while eating: focus on each bite, avoid TV or phone during meals. You may eat too much without knowing it.

STRATEGY 5: GET ACTIVE, STAY ACTIVE

You can lose weight without exercise, but it's harder. Regular physical activity helps burn off extra calories and has many other benefits: lifts mood, lowers blood pressure, helps you sleep better, and helps keep off the weight you lose.

Exercise recommendations:
- Work up to at least 30 minutes of aerobic exercise most days of the week.
- Aim to do strength training exercises at least twice a week (lift weights, use exercise bands, do pushups).

Ways to move more throughout the day:
- Use the stairs instead of an elevator.
- Park at the far end of the lot.
- Stand instead of sit while on the phone.
- Walk laps or march in place while watching TV.

STRATEGY 6: CHANGE YOUR MINDSET

It's not enough to eat healthy foods and exercise for just a few weeks or months. To keep off extra weight, make these healthy changes a way of life. Think about negative habits or other challenges that have kept you from losing weight in the past. Plan for how you'll deal with them going forward.

You'll likely have some setbacks. Don't give up — simply start fresh the next day. Remember that you're planning to change your life. It won't happen all at once.
