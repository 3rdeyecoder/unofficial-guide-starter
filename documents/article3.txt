TITLE: 12 Simple Ways to Break Through a Weight Loss Plateau
SOURCE: Healthline
MEDICALLY REVIEWED BY: Daniel Bubnis, M.S., NASM-CPT, NASE Level II-CSS
DATE: Updated February 26, 2024
========================================================================

While weight tends to come off fairly rapidly at first, at some point it can seem as though your weight won't budge. This inability to lose more weight after initially successful weight loss is known as a weight loss plateau or stall. Here are 12 tips to break through it.

1. CUT BACK ON CARBS

There is some evidence that eating a low carb diet may help reduce hunger. This may lead you to subconsciously eat less, making it easier to begin losing weight again without hunger or discomfort. Whether carb restriction leads to a "metabolic advantage" that causes your body to burn more calories continues to be debated among experts.

2. INCREASE EXERCISE FREQUENCY OR INTENSITY

Your metabolic rate slows as you lose weight. As weight declines, the progressive reduction in metabolic rate can make continued weight loss difficult. Exercise has been shown to help counteract this effect. Research suggests that for losing weight, fat, and belly fat, aerobic training is more effective than resistance training — though a combination is best for overall health.

Non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT) — fidgeting, changing posture, and similar activities — can have a major effect on your metabolic rate. An easy way to increase your NEAT is by standing up more, including using a standing desk.

3. TRACK EVERYTHING YOU EAT

Research suggests people have a tendency to underestimate the amount of food they eat. Tracking your calories and macronutrients — protein, fat, and carbs — provides concrete information about how much you're taking in. The act of recording your food intake alone may improve your weight loss efforts.

4. DON'T SKIMP ON PROTEIN

Increasing your protein intake may help. Protein boosts metabolic rate more than fat or carbs — protein digestion boosts calorie burning by 20–30%, which is more than twice as much as fat or carbs. Protein also stimulates the production of hormones that help reduce appetite and make you feel full and satisfied.

5. MANAGE STRESS

Stress can often put the brakes on weight loss. In addition to promoting comfort eating and triggering food cravings, stress increases your body's production of cortisol — the "stress hormone." Too much cortisol can increase belly fat storage. Research has shown that learning to manage stress can help promote weight loss.

6. TRY INTERMITTENT FASTING

Intermittent fasting involves going for long periods of time without eating, typically between 16–48 hours. It has been credited with promoting the loss of body fat and weight. Common methods include: 16:8 (fasting 16 hours, eating within an 8-hour window), 5:2 (very low calories 2 days per week), and alternate-day fasting.

7. AVOID ALCOHOL

Although one alcoholic drink (4 oz of wine, 1.5 oz of hard liquor, or 12 oz of beer) contains only around 100 calories, it provides no nutritional value. Alcohol loosens inhibitions, which may lead you to overeat or make poor food choices. Research has shown that alcohol can suppress fat burning and may lead to belly fat accumulation. One study of 283 adults found that reducing alcohol intake led to a reduction in overeating and greater weight loss.

8. EAT MORE FIBER

Soluble fiber (the type that dissolves in water or liquid) slows the movement of food through your digestive tract, helping you feel full and satisfied. Another way fiber may aid weight loss is by decreasing the number of calories you absorb from other foods.

9. DRINK WATER, COFFEE, OR TEA

Plain water can boost metabolism, which may translate into weight loss over time, especially when consumed before meals (which may reduce food intake). Green tea contains an antioxidant known as EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) that may promote weight loss. Caffeinated beverages can significantly enhance the metabolism-boosting and fat-burning effects of exercise.

10. GET PLENTY OF SLEEP

Not getting enough sleep can lead to weight gain by lowering your metabolic rate and altering hormone levels to drive appetite and fat storage. Aim for 7–8 hours of sleep per night to support weight loss and overall health.

11. EAT VEGETABLES AT EVERY MEAL

Most vegetables are low in calories and carbs, high in fiber, and loaded with beneficial nutrients. Research has found that diets that include lots of vegetables tend to produce the greatest weight loss.

12. DON'T RELY ON THE SCALE ALONE

Scale reading may not always accurately reflect your progress. If you're working out regularly, you may be building muscle, which is denser than fat and takes up less room. So if the scale weight isn't moving, you could be building muscle and losing fat while maintaining a stable weight. Also, you may retain water for various reasons including hormonal changes.

FAQ:

What causes a weight loss plateau?
Various reasons for hitting a plateau include overeating, not eating enough protein, not getting enough exercise, and not getting enough sleep.

How long does a weight loss plateau last?
How long a weight loss plateau lasts varies from person to person — it can be a few weeks or a few months.

Can a cheat day break a weight loss plateau?
There is some evidence that an occasional, small break from dieting can lead to greater overall weight loss in some cases.

BOTTOM LINE:

Weight loss plateaus are a typical part of the weight loss process — nearly everyone experiences a stall at some point. Several strategies can help you begin to lose weight again and safely achieve your goal weight.
